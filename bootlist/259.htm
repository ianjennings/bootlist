2) CRC CheckSum Error

Samantha wrote - I 'm trying to download an AntiVirus program and keep on getting a CRC CheckSum Error when I click on the file to install it. What gives?


*** Most commonly its a bad download. eg that message usually means the file was corrupted during download, or, could NOT be saved to your pc correctly. It often happens when file servers are too busy, [overloaded] or low quality. Or, it can happen if you just have too many background apps running, esp. apps that phone home [often spyware] while you download a large file, tho less rare.

Try again from a different download server, and make sure to delete your Internet cache and temp/tmp folders. And make sure your pc is spyware free. Also, many webmasters are now doing pages where they recommend NOT using a download manager as that can mess up the download. For example, see the "Please note" on this page:

http://www.cybertechhelp.com/download/file/windows-2000-server-bootdisk

In summary, if you get a CRC error, it generally means one or several "packets" have been lost. More on packets here:

http://computer.howstuffworks.com/question525.htm

To put it in laymans terms, When a file is "shipped" to somebody, its NOT all shipped in a single big box. Rather, it's broken up into many thousands of tiny boxes, and if only a single tiny box is lost during shipment, the full file cannot be reconstructed properly on your PC.

6) Smoke And Mirrors

Charles B. wrote - Many, but not all, photos [on webpages I visit] display with crude pixelation, rather than displaying with fine resolution.


*** Perhaps you are using an "acceleration" program from your ISP, which, to "speed up" downloads, does not allow you to download the entire file [graphic] as posted on the webpage. Rather, it "reformats" the image to a less intense graphic to "simulate" a faster download time.

ie Smoke and mirrors to make you "believe" your download time is really faster.

Malke said:
Turn off your ISP's acceleration program. Right now you're not downloading the full amount of the pictures' data.
