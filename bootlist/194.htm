2) More On Recording Sound/Music

Harold S. emailed:

[I'd like to comment on] Issue 193 4) How To Record A Webcast From A Radio Station

For recording sound on the computer from it seems anywhere, Record from Audio Input in CDex:

http://cdexos.sourceforge.net/

It captures any sound going thru the computer. I just used it to record the Hitchhiker's Guide 3rd Phase. Note - You can NOT have anything else in the computer generating sound or it will pick it up at the same time. (eg. Beeps from other programs, help agents from MS OFFICE, etc.)

The main thing I do normally use it for is to record my tapes into the computer and then to split the large mp3 files into separate tracks via another program:

http://www.mpesch3.de/

The mp3 splitter is a great tool.

3) Most Excellent Guestbook Service

The guestbook service I was using for years went kerplunk and I was even paying for it. So I spent lots of time finding another one that met my needs and I now use:

http://www.alxnet.com/

Features:

Free
Allows you to choose the fields and create two custom fields
No banner ads
Bad word and IP filters
Multiple post delete
Can add your own graphics
Reliable, fast, and easy to use interface

I just figure that if you're in the market for a guestbook that you may want to take advantage of my time searching and testing out perhaps 20 or so guestbooks. It was quite an annoying task, as most of them dont list all the features until you sign up and play with it. And many of the pay for ones dont have all the features of this free one. Or, you may just want to save this link for later use to save you the time I had to spend when I lost my guestbook.
