1) COM Port Resources

Betsy Sue asks - Hi. I love to fix old computers to help my neighbors out and I think it's real cool that you write about Windows XP problems and hardware problems that any fix-it-person may encounter. I am a long time reader of your newsletter and I remember you writing about com port addresses and resources. You had a nice list but I cant seem to find it. I must have left it in one of my neighbors trailers.

When I mean old I mean real old like Windows 95 computers with 16 megs of RAM.


*** That was back in December 2002. Guess it's a good time to repost the list.

COM1 3F8 IRQ4
COM2 2F8 IRQ3
COM3 3E8 IRQ4
COM4 2E8 IRQ3

This list was actually the first page in my data book for many years. It was invaluable when you had to set up addresses and resources on old controller cards and/or modems using the jumpers.

ps Remember when Device Manager actually was a device manager and let you choose your settings to resolve resource problems? [grin]

Ric writes - I got infected by a virus and used system restore to get the machine running again. I've cleaned up the virus, but it still exists within the restore point. Is there any way to delete the restore point?

Carey F. answers:
One cannot selectively delete restore points. When you turnoff System Restore, all restore points will be deleted. This is the only way to remove any virus files that have infected System Restore files.

How to Turn On and Turn Off System Restore in Windows XP:
http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=310405

3) S-Video In Laptop Not Working

GabrielG writes - I just bought a S video cable for my laptop and is connected to my TV but it's not working at all. Am missing something else ? What Do I have to do to view my Laptop screen to my TV ?

Brad G. answers:
Usually you have to turn on the S-Video when you hook something up to it. Most laptops I've seen use a keystroke such as Fn-F6 to do this. If your Fn key is purple then you should see a little purple tv icon on one of your keys (it's F6 on mine).

Another possibility might be to go to Control Panel -> Display and check on the Settings tab. You might be able to enable it through there.

4) Used Laptop Advice

Buffy inquires - Could someone recommend a good source to purchase one of these? Second not being able to splash on a new one could someone tell me whether say a Pentium 3 with 256 Ram would effectively run WinXP?

UTF replies:
Factor in the cost of a replacement battery (which can be very expensive) when buying a secondhand laptop. Many have batteries that will run for a few minutes at most [which is often one reason folks are selling them from my experience].

Presumably *just* running XP isn't the issue - it is whether it will give acceptable performance running your intended applications. In which case, saying what you intent to use the laptop for would be useful!

Bear in mind also that you may find that the laptop is not fully functional using XP, an operating system it was not designed for - the PC card may not work, the graphics adapter may only work in a basic way, the fax-modem may not work at all, etc. I have three laptops here that suffer from this, even though XP appears to run ok at first glance.

You need to research this thoroughly, once you know which make/model you intend to buy.


*** I tend to agree with UTF. Laptop makers often dont, or cant update drivers like they do in desktops. The hardware is way more proprietary and laptops are primarily designed to run the OS they came with new. Of course there are exceptions, as always, to any general statement, even tho it's based on lots of personal experience.

Another thing to watch out for is dead pixels on the screen. One or two may not seem to matter at first but they can really get to annoy you after a while. And, you may not even see them until you start using your favorite app.

Then there are worn out tracking buttons, system passwords that have to be professionally removed by an authorized dealer, and worn out AC/battery charging hardware. So if you're buying online make sure in your agreement you demand that all these things work to cover yourself in case you have to cancel the credit card charge.

5) chkdsk Not Working

spike writes - Whenever I try to use checkdisk my computer restarts and I receive the message:

[volume is ntfs cannot open volume for direct access] And checkdisk doesn't run . Any ideas?

Richard replies:
If this is the first time you have run CHKDSK since installing Zone Alarm 5 that is where your problem comes from. Go to the FAQs at the ZA site and follow the instructions to uninstall ZA5 and replace with ZA 4.5. There are other possible causes but ZA5 is flavour of the month.

Alex N. adds:
It only arises when something goes wrong so that CHKDSK gets set to run at Startup. At that point the trouble with ZA kicks in. You need to go back to the earlier ZA, then the CHKDSK will get in to do its stuff.


*** Zone Alarm is starting to get a little to bloated for my taste. It's already about twice the size of other firewalls.
