1) Intellipoint Mouse Drivers

Moon said - Just about to rebuild and was wondering if there are still issues with some of the intellipoint software and xpsp2? I have a natural keyboard pro and an optical [MS] Intellimouse. [Where do I get the correct drivers?]

Jim B. responds:

2) XP Folders Do Not Retain View Settings

Shiva posted - I recently found that my folders are not retaining their view settings. View all folders and remember folder settings have been duly checked. This started to happen only recently. Does not bother me but is intriguing. A thorough check for mal-ware was made - none found.

Phil answers:
XP [Original] only remembers 400 folder views by default. To have it remember more:

First download and install the fix to make xp remember more than 8000 folders:
Column 1, #2
http://www.kellys-korner-xp.com/xp_tweaks.htm

Then, in explorer, set your view as you like and go to: tools, folder options, view tab, apply to all folders. Also make sure to check: remember each folder's view settings.

Will D. adds:
The folder settings limit have been increased to 5000 in SP2.

Jon buds in:
This link may help:

http://www.tweakxp.com/tweak1922.aspx

5) Files In The FOUND Folder

Jess F. inquired - I have a gazillion files in Explorer in folders named FOUND. What are they (dumps?) and can I nuke them?

Will D. answers:
I think you'll find they are folders created by chkdsk.

R. McCarty says:
The are recovered fragments, that result from a run of Chkdsk. Similar to the FATxx type of Cross-Linked files and other data disjointed from the allocation table. Continued recovery of these data pieces may indicate an issue with your disk drive. If you [have] irreplaceable data [that] isn't backed up, do it today.


*** Can often be caused by bad or incompatible ram as well right after you add it. Yes the files can be nuked. When you tweak your ram problem they can/should go away [eg not be generated] assuming it was due to ram that XP and/or your motherboard didn't like. They can also be generated by shutting down your PC improperly.
