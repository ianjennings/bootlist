1) Invalid Entries In XP Add/Remove

Monte C. asks - I have a bunch of programs in my ADD REMOVE PROGRAMS list that I uninstalled but are still showing up in the list. Is there a way to get rid of these?

Wesley V. answered:
Remove invalid entries in Add/Remove Programs:
http://windowsxp.mvps.org/AddRemove.htm

How to Manually Remove Programs from the Add or Remove Programs Tool:
http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=314481

Stan said:
First, start the Registry Editor by going to Start, Run and typing RegEdit Navigate to:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Uninstall.

You'll see a list of all the programs available in your Add/Remove Programs Control Panel. You can simply select an entry from the list on the left, press the Delete key, and confirm to remove it. Close the Registry Editor and restart Windows. The item will be gone from your Add/Remove Programs list.

3) Problem Burning Bootable CDs

Janet inquired - I never, or hardly, ever have a problem making cds or music cds or saving data to cds. But why is it I seem to have problems making bootable cds? They seem to create with no errors but then they do not boot or get an error message "reading drive a:" while the finished bootable cd is starting. Or sometimes it boots fine and I get the same error again. This doesn't make any sense.


*** As you might guess, I spend lots of time creating bootable CDs. Here's what I've learned:

1. I often find that I have to set the burning speed real low when doing bootables. 8X seems to be best.

2. The BRAND of the media is often the problem. In other words, the brand you use may just not like playing with bootable files on your pc.

3. The AGE of the media may be the problem. In other words, I have a buddy who stocked up on on CD-R but when he went to CD-RW he gave me all types of still shrink-wrapped 5 year old spindles. My kids love all the free media, but they often give me problems for bootables.

4. I often have to close down all the background apps prior to creating bootable CDs, or even creating a bootable CD from an ISO. By background apps I mean the ones in the system tray which can be temporarily disabled using msconfig.

5. By default when creating bootable CDs I make sure that the disk is also closed so no further data may be written to it. Sometimes I forget and that's the reason for the coaster.

In summary, I'm not pretending to be a CD burning expert, this is just my experience and what I try if I'm getting coasters playing with bootables. Like Janet, I rarely have a problem at all with just plain burning using any media, speed, program, etc.
