1) Windows Crash Info

Alex C. asked - I was restarting the PC after hibernate recently and I got a Blue Screen. I rebooted. Where can I find any log info about this crash?

Doug K. answered:
The file will be in C:\Windows\Minidump

Daniel M. added:
Simply open Start, Run, type (without quotes) '%SystemRoot%\Minidump' and click OK. The minidump files will appear on the screen.

2) Make Sure You Get A CD With Your PC

davej678 wrote - I need to reinstall my windows xp operating system and start from scratch. The problem is, I dont have the xp disc because it (windows xp) was already pre-installed on the system when I purchased it. what can I do? My computer manufacturer was no help. I could not get a hold of a real person to talk to.

Ken B. answered:
OEM vendors are required by their agreement with Microsoft to give you a means of reinstalling, should it be necessary. They can do this in one of three ways:

1. An OEM copy of Windows

2. A restore CD

3. A hidden partition on your drive, with restore information.

If you don't have 1 or 2, you should have 3, but you should contact your vendor or check your system documentation to find out.

Personally, I find both 2 and 3 unacceptable, and would never choose to buy a computer that came with an operating system unless I got a complete generic installation CD for that operating system.


*** If your OEM PC maker provided you with Item 3 above and your hard drive crashes, then you may have to buy XP again to acquire a CD to get your system back up. However, since you did pay for XP when you bought your system, even tho it you didn't get the disk, I'd certainly first request that the vender supply the ways and means to install XP again on your new drive.

3) Stop Unwanted USB "Error" Notice

Bill asked - I sometimes get a caption in the tray that one of my usb devices is not working. I don't want to be reminded of this. How do I stop this alert from coming up ?

Daniel M. responded:
1. Open Start, Run, type (without quotes) 'devmgmt.msc' and click OK.
2. Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers.
3. Double-click your USB controller.
4. Select the Advanced tab.
5. Check the box 'Don't tell me about USB errors'.


*** You may see what looks like a _parent_ controller and several, 2-4, sons/daughters. Do the parent first. If no joy then also do the sons/daughters. One can always change it back if you dont like the results.
