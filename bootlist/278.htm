2) What About RAID 0?

Mr User wrote - I'm about to setup a SATA 2x250GB RAID 0 system. Obviously to increase access speed. I'm curious how partitions would be handled. Would each partition be allocated a portion of each drive or would it simply be a contiguous area?

Ron M replied:
In a Raid 0 configuration (data striping) all files, folders, and partitions are spread across the drives.

You realize, I hope, that while this may provide some modest gains in performance, such as during the system startup, it also substantially increases your risk with regard to disk failures and it is therefore important to ensure that you have adequate backup and recovery procedures in place.

In a RAID 0 configuration a failure of any of the drives results in the total loss of the content of all of the drives and there is no way to recover anything.

Assume for the purposes of illustration that there is a 10% probability that a given disk drive will fail within the first 3 years of usage. With 2 such drives in a RAID 0 configuration there is a 19% probability that either of them will fail during those 3 years.


*** More info:
http://www.pcguide.com/ref/hdd/perf/raid/levels/singleLevel0-c.html

3) Show Both Time, Day, And Date In Bottom Tray

Mr. Hennings posted - I want my PC to show both the date, day, and time in the tray. I know I can do this by expanding the tray to two or more lines but there has to be a better way.

6) How To Disable Popup Tips

Jeremy W. wrote - How do you get rid of those rectangular light brown boxes that appear whenever the cursor is allowed to rest on anything of any importance? I find them annoying beyond belief and have tried everything to get rid of them with no success.


*** If you are speaking of what's called "balloon tips" here are some links/fixes:

http://www.dougknox.com/xp/scripts_desc/xp_balloontips.htm
http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=307729

Rhonda Lea K. added:
Download Tweak UI from Microsoft:

http://www.microsoft.com/windowsxp/downloads/powertoys/xppowertoys.mspx

Choose "Taskbar and Start menu" and then untick the box for "Enable balloon tips."
