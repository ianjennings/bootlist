2) Blurry JPGs. Is It The DPI Or Other?

Ben Rum inquired - I am seeing JPEG's appear blurry, as if not fully downloaded, in some places on my PC. Mainly Internet Explorer. Even the "Start", "Shutdown" and "Stand By" buttons appear this way. However, if I save a website JPEG locally, and view it in Windows Picture & Fax Viewer it appears normal. Is there some OS setting that does this?

The PC is a Dell laptop (Latitude D505) and I'm not sure if its an WinXP setting or PC Setting.

Vanguard answers:
Does the image look clearer when you hit the Refresh toolbar button? If so, that is probably the wonderful accelerator program you got from your ISP. It downloads only half of the picture's pixels to give you a faster download of the page but a fuzzier one. Great idea, huh, to lie to their customers that they are getting an accelerated connection rate (and might even charge for it) but really are only delivering a partial version of each image? Heck, you could speed you connection up even more if you blocked images completely so as not to waste any bandwidth and time to download them.

If the above isn't your problem, check the DPI setting for your screen. Right-click on desktop and select Properties, or run the Display applet from Control Panel, Settings panel, Advanced button, General tab, set the DPI (96 is the default, I believe).

The specs for LCD screens usually list a "native" resolution (i.e., the resolution at which the screen has the correct number of pixels without having to use interpolation to emulate other resolutions). See if switching to the native resolution clears up your screen.
