5) USB Drive Letter Problem

Rich inquired - Like others I have a number of usb devices. When I plug a media card or memory stick into the usb [port] nothing opens even though all the drivers are loaded for these devices. I get the audio that a device has been plugged in but cannot find it.

I have to reboot with the device plugged in and I will find it under My Computer in Network Drives as a disconnected network drive. Once I double click it will open and show the contents of the card or stick. [Is there a fix?]

CW answers:
I believe this is the usual cause.

Quote.
This problem occurs if you map a network drive to the first available drive letter following the drive letters for the local volumes and CD/DVD drives. When you install a new device or volume, a software called Mount Manager tries to assign drive letters to the new volumes. But since the software does not recognize mapped drive letters, it assigns the next available local drive letter to the new device. This causes a collision with the existing mapped network drive.

http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=297694


*** In other words, if you want to map a network drive(s) then choose a drive letter(s) way down the alphabet. I usually assign mapped drive letters X, Y, and Z. Makes it easier to use in an office or small business setup as well.

On a related issue, this reminds me of why I always strongly suggest that you assign out of the way drive letters for your CDrom drives. For example, I always setup pcs so the cd reader is drive R: and the CD writer, if one has 2 drives, is drive W:. This puts them out of the way so when you add second hard drive or hard drive partitions [resulting in new drive letters] then it wont conflict with the drive letter of your CDrom drive.

By conflict, yes I know that if you add a new HDD the CDrom drives will simply be assigned the next letters(s) inline. But, that change may affect some software that you have installed that is used to looking for a certain CDrom letter eg software that requires that you have the CD in to play a game.

In summary, setup your pc to permanently assign drive letters for CDrom drives or Mapped drives in the latter part of the alphabet. This way when you add new USB or other temporary devices that need a drive letter you minimize the chance of running into conflicts or niggles.
